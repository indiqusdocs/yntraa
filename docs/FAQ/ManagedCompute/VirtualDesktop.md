# 

# Frequently Asked Question


<details><summary>**What is Virtual Desktop-as-a-Service (vDaaS)?**</summary>vDaaS is a cloud-based service that delivers secure, fully functional Windows or Linux desktops to users over the internet, supporting flexible, remote, and hybrid work across all devices. Unlike traditional PCs, these desktops are hosted in secure data centers, eliminating on-site hardware and enabling quick, scalable desktop infrastructure management.</details>

<details><summary>**How does vDaaS improve workforce productivity?**</summary>vDaaS allows employees to securely access their work environment—including applications and data—from anywhere and any device, streamlining onboarding, supporting hybrid/remote work, and reducing IT support complexity.</details>

<details><summary>**What are the main benefits of vDaaS?**</summary>Lower total cost of ownership, improved security, scalability, centralised management, device independence, and rapid deployment for changing business needs.</details>

<details><summary>**How is vDaaS different from traditional desktops?**</summary>Traditional desktops require local hardware and maintenance. vDaaS is cloud hosted, reducing IT costs, enabling greater flexibility, and delivering stronger security.</details>

<details><summary>**What types of businesses benefit from vDaaS?**</summary>SMBs and enterprises across industries like BPO, BFSI, education, media, and healthcare—especially those with remote or hybrid workforces—benefit from DaaS, which can be tailored to various roles and workloads.</details>

<details><summary>**Which desktop models are available with vDaaS?**</summary>vDaaS offers both Hosted Shared Desktop(multi-user, non-persistent) and Personal Desktop (dedicated, persistent) models to meet different business requirements.</details>

<details><summary>**What is a Hosted Shared Desktop?**</summary>A Hosted Shared Desktop allows users to connect to any available shared desktop at login. These environments are best for standardized tasks, are non-persistent, and generally do not retain user-specific changes across sessions.</details>

<details><summary>**What is a Personal Desktop?**</summary>A Personal Desktop provides each user with a unique, persistent virtual machine, so user preferences and data persist between sessions. These are ideal for users needing custom configurations or administrative rights.</details>

<details><summary>**How to choose between Hosted Shared Desktops and Personal Desktops?**</summary>Hosted Shared Desktops are best for task workers and cost efficiency; Personal Desktops suit knowledge workers requiring persistent data, custom applications, or admin access.</details>

<details><summary>**Which desktop operating systems are supported?**</summary>Windows 11 Enterprise and Windows Server OS, with planned support for Linux distributions like CentOS and Ubuntu.</details>

<details><summary>**What devices can be used?**</summary>Desktops, laptops, tablets, and smartphones via dedicated workspace apps or HTML5-compatible browsers.</details>

<details><summary>**What types of applications and peripherals are supported?**</summary>vDaaS supports standard office and business applications, web and SaaS apps, and many peripherals such as USB, printers, multi-monitors, and scanners (some require admin approval or special clients).</details>

<details><summary>**Is cloud storage integration available?**</summary>Yes, integration with Google Drive, Dropbox, Box, and OneDrive is supported.</details>

<details><summary>**Can vDaaS be integrated with existing IT systems?**</summary>Yes, including Active Directory, on-prem/cloud integration, and third-party identity providers (Okta, Ping, ADFS, SAML 2.0).</details>

<details><summary>**Is vDaaS compliant with industry security standards?**</summary>Yes, with encryption, multi-factor authentication (MFA), single sign-on (SSO), role based access (RBAC), audit logging, and anti-malware. Compliance dashboards and governance tools are included.</details>

<details><summary>**Are backup and disaster recovery options available?**</summary>Additional persistent storage and backup services are offered as add-ons for business continuity and data protection.</details>

<details><summary>**What governance tools are provided?**</summary>Role-based access control, compliance/health dashboards, activity and audit logging, and policy enforcement for patching/alerts/change management.</details>

<details><summary>**What are the billing options for vDaaS plans?**</summary>Pay-as-you-go, fixed term (1 or 3 years), and flexible saving plans with monthly or annual billing options.</details>

<details><summary>**Does Yntraa charge for network bandwidth?**</summary>Standard plans include 1000 GB of combined ingress and egress. Usage beyond this limit is billed at addon rates.</details>

<details><summary>**What are the prerequisites for using vDaaS?**</summary>A thin client or low-end device, stable internet connectivity, sufficient bandwidth, and compatible applications.</details>

<details><summary>**What SLAs and support are available?**</summary>24x7 technical support is included. SLAs cover uptime, response time, and issue resolution, with exclusions for scheduled maintenance, customer-imposed restrictions, and force majeure events.</details>

<details><summary>**Are there free trials or promotional offers?**</summary>Yes, a free trial with usage credits may be available. Check current promotional terms for details.</details>

<details><summary>**Who is the point of contact for support?**</summary>Depending on your plan, either a shared or dedicated Technical Account Manager or the Cloud Helpdesk Team is assigned.</details>

<details><summary>**Can vDaaS be deployed on-premises or in other clouds?**</summary>Yes, deployment is supported both on public clouds and within your own data center as per business needs.</details>

<details><summary>**Are APIs available for automation and integration?**</summary>vDaaS offers REST APIs for workflow automation and integration with enterprise IT systems.</details>
